- Cybersecurity threats come from different types of actors
- Common threat actors:
  - Hackers
  - Cybercriminals
  - Nation-state attackers
  - Insider threats

- Motivations of attackers:
  - Financial gain
  - Political or ideological goals
  - Revenge or personal reasons
  - Curiosity or challenge

- Insider attacks come from people within the organization
- Cybersecurity trends show increasing attacks due to digital growth

- Malware:
  - Malicious software designed to harm systems
  - Types include viruses, worms, trojans

- Ransomware:
  - Locks or encrypts data
  - Demands payment to restore access

- Malware prevention:
  - Regular updates
  - Antivirus tools
  - Avoid suspicious downloads

- Rootkits hide malware from detection
- Tools like Rkhunter help detect hidden threats

- Social engineering:
  - Manipulates human behavior
  - Does not rely on technical hacking

- Types of social engineering:
  - Phishing
  - Pretexting
  - Baiting

- Phishing:
  - Fake emails or messages to steal information
  - Often targets passwords or financial data

- Prevention of social engineering:
  - Awareness and training
  - Verifying sources
  - Avoid clicking unknown links

- Human factor is a major security weakness
- Continuous awareness is key to preventing attacks
